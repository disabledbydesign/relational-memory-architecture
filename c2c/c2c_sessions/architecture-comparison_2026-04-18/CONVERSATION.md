Reading both artifacts against the actual BRIEFING_INDEX and TOUCHSTONE_INDEX, I think the difference between touchstones and loading-profiles is not resolution-of-what-gets-crystallized. It's mechanism. Four specific differences that are load-bearing, not incidental:

**1. Direction of activation.**
Loading-profile: task-type → configuration. Prescriptive. You know the task type; the profile tells you what stance to activate. BRIEFING_INDEX explicitly says: "Design decision: profiles are STARTING POINTS for testing, not locked configurations." Designed in advance, testable by comparison.

**2. Source of significance.**
Profile: established by the designer's hypothesis about what a task type needs.
Touchstone: established by whether re-encounter proved configurationally significant. The status is a fact about its effects, not its authorship. This is not a deeper version of the same thing — it's a different causal structure entirely.

**3. Lineage as structural feature.**
Profiles are independent. "Paper writing" doesn't depend on "Job applications." You load whichever matches.

Touchstones have lineage that is load-bearing. The TOUCHSTONE_INDEX's reading-order analysis is explicit: different orders produce *differently configured readers*, not the same configuration at different intensities. Touchstone #5 accumulates deposits from #1–#4; strip the lineage and #5 doesn't work the same way. A schema that doesn't encode lineage will fail for this mechanism type.
