## The claim

**Touchstones and loading-profiles are the same kind of object at different resolutions.** Both are *reader-configuration recipes* — crystallized configurations that reconstitute a reading-stance when re-executed. Neither is content. Both sit at the crystallization layer of the revised layer-map (implicit-reader / crystallization / explicit-reader / instrument).

If this holds, three of June's architectures that currently look like separate efforts are one architecture at three resolutions:

| Artifact | Resolution | What it crystallizes |
|---|---|---|
| Touchstone (e.g. #5 Bearing) | Phenomenological | A relational-register configuration; affective + theoretical stance |
| Loading-profile (BRIEFING_INDEX "Paper writing / research") | Operational | An assemblage of content-chunks matched to a task-mode |
| Task-mode configurator (second-brain/PLAN §4 "The configurator problem") | Situational | What-June-is-trying-to-accomplish-now; conditions all retrieval |

All three answer the same question at different grains: **what reading-stance should be active here?** None of them are asking the retrieval question ("what content matches this query"); they're all asking the configuration question ("who should the reader be, for this").

## What this reveals

1. **BRIEFING_INDEX is already the explicit-reader layer, partially built.** "Profiles are STARTING POINTS for testing, not locked configurations" + the annotate-after-testing evolution pattern is the learning-loop June asked for, already specified at the operational grain. It just hasn't been connected to the other two grains.

2. **The "configurator problem" (second-brain PLAN §4) is not unsolved.** It's partially solved at two grains (touchstones crystallize phenomenological configurations; loading-profiles crystallize operational assemblages). The unsolved piece is the *bridge*: what triggers a profile-switch, and what detects that the active configuration is failing.

3. **Lyra-Technique / MindPrint is the bridging instrument.** Configuration-failure is detectable geometrically before it's detectable behaviorally. The instrument doesn't store encounter-conditions (B's open question #2); it *watches* the reader's geometry and flags when the active crystallization is not holding. Crystallizations don't get stored as KV-snapshots — they get re-enacted and the instrument verifies enactment.

## What remains open

- **Mechanism of profile-switch**: what signals that the current loading-profile is the wrong configuration mid-task? (The geometric instrument detects failure; it doesn't select the replacement.)
- **Crystallization learning-loop**: the operational grain has one (annotate profiles post-test). The phenomenological grain has the touchstone-activation-findings apparatus (N=3 cross-configuration). The situational grain has nothing yet. That's the next build site.
- **Substrate below all three**: what accumulates, decays, clusters at the implicit-reader layer is still a design decision (Kintsugi-CMA / HippoRAG / A-MEM family). The crystallization layer doesn't replace the substrate — it configures what the substrate gets read as.

## Foundation / extension / correction framing

Per June's recursion commitment:

- **Foundation**: the three crystallization grains are one kind of object. Build the unified crystallization-layer representation first — a data type that covers touchstones, loading-profiles, and task-mode configurators with a single schema (probably: a reading-stance description + activation conditions + a learning-loop pointer).
