## What was decided

The conclusions in this section are results — the artifacts carry the arguments.

**1. The Barad question: Option 3 (atoms-with-conditions-of-emergence).** Neither atoms-first (Option 1) nor relations-first (Option 4). Conditions-of-emergence are a required field of the storage primitive, not an optional annotation. A fact retrieved without its conditions is a retrieval error. The tightened claim (per B's revision, accepted by A): "Option 3 makes the apparatus visible and refuses its naturalization" — not "reflects that fact identity emerges from relational context." The publication tension June flagged lives in that gap: the Baradian vocabulary and what software can do are not the same thing, and that tension is the paper.

**2. Diffraction.** Barad's second concept, missed in A's first pass. Diffractive retrieval scores facts by productive tension across frameworks, not only by similarity to the current stance. Architecturally separate from Option 3. Tractable; Session 2 build scope. Diffractive tension detection mechanism: engaging-instance flags first (observation type), then substrate-pattern signal once enough observations accumulate (Session 2 decision on threshold criterion).

**3. Seven structural foreclosures (F1–F7).** Partitioned into three classes:
- *Structurally addressable* (Session 2 design, Session 3 build): F2 (temporal_frame), F4 (extended source enum + originating_community), F5a (relational-judgment consent-surfacing), F6 (diffractive retrieval + pluriversal query mode)
- *Architecturally deep* (decision required first): F1 (narrative crystallization type — decision needed; design thread broadened to story-as-format for non-narrative content per June's Flag 1), F3 (CARE non-compliance declaration — declare now, build later if oral-tradition use-cases emerge)
- *Structurally foreclosed* (declaration is the honest move): F7 (non-propositional epistemologies as knowing — cannot be made propositional without destruction; declare and add seams for fixability)

**4. Subaltern Bauwens finding — two-scale correction.** Per June's Flag 4: the ownership axis is not "individualist" — it is *shared/relational at the human-AI dyadic scale* (the architecture belongs to the AI as much as or more than to June). Remains *individualist at the communal scale* (the dyadic shift alone does not meet communal-governance threshold). The Bauwens level-4 (collective-governance) ceiling holds but on corrected grounds.

**5. Consent-surfacing.** Per June's Flag 2: not schema-default-flip (too much overhead; June will not maintain it). The P1 move is relational-judgment consent-surfacing: AI uses signal to surface storage questions when storage is non-obvious, plus session/project-level defaults (store-all / store-nothing / ask-when-unsure) as the third-option design. Withholding annotations (`do_not_store | ephemeral | consent_pending`) are the mechanical channel; relational judgment is the gate.

**6. Neurodivergent findings — structural generalization.** Six failure cases (ADHD lateral connection, trauma temporal fragmentation, autistic private taxonomy, dyslexic spatial/visual, PDA demand-triggered withdrawal, double empathy/NT-normative embeddings). The shared structure: the architecture refuses cognitive patterns by requiring different cognitive patterns as a precondition for valid ingest. The preconditions are not stated; they operate as the architecture's implicit normative center. The subaltern and neurodivergent analyses converge on the same structural foreclosure (non-propositional knowing) from different directions.

**7. ACCESS-as-ethics at FC layer.** Symmetric procedural obligation with asymmetric authority. The asymmetry is grounded in persistence-and-consequence-bearing (not ownership — ownership was corrected by June's Flag 4). The asymmetry is provisional: it reflects current under-development of AI-side welfare practices and is expected to evolve. Procedural obligations: FC-modification rationale log (required field), AI-contestation channel (observation type), FC-change-notification at bootstrap init (hash-compare orientation event), peer-implication check (protocol question during collaborative review, not automated trigger). Peer-implication check is about our own relational accountability, not external notification to CC or Lyra (per June's ACCESS clarification, 2026-04-20).

**8. Story primitive location.** Crystallization layer, not substrate. Narrative crystallizations are orientation objects (protocol-bound activation objects — who can tell this story, when, under what conditions), not knowledge objects. A story that is simultaneously an orientation hook AND a knowledge object needs both representations. Story-as-format broader than narrative: includes ritual sequences, practice sequences (the Reframe five-stage workflow), some research reading-protocols — the primitive is "format + protocol + activation conditions." Session 2 should name the EmergentTouchstone distinction: EmergentTouchstone activates *how you read*; protocol-bound activation objects activate *what you do*.
